Proposal — Contexts & rules, made legible. Addresses the dogfooding feedback: the model wasn't clear, there was no validation, and input was blind free-text.
  • Pick from your apps, with icons — “+ Add context” / ✎ Edit open a searchable grid of the apps on your Mac. No typing names blind. (try it)
  • Unsorted apps — a new section surfaces apps you’ve actually used that aren’t sorted yet, with one‑click Assign. Works from day one, before you’ve set anything up.
  • Conflicts are caught — put an app in two contexts and it warns you which one wins (try adding Slack to Deep work).
  • Plain language + a live preview — matching is explained in words, and you see what a rule catches before saving. Power‑user title: patterns moved to an “Advanced” drawer.

Unsorted apps

Apps you’ve used that aren’t in a context yet. Their time is still counted — it just shows as Other on the dial until you sort it.

Contexts & rules

A context is a colour bucket on your dial. The apps under it are sorted there automatically. Each app belongs to one context — if two could claim it, the first one wins (we’ll warn you).
Edit any context and everything re-sorts. Nothing is recaptured.
