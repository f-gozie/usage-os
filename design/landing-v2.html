How it works · 01

The whole day, one dial.

A 24-hour dial, midnight at the top. Your hours inked in plain colors, so a glance tells you where they went — deep work through the morning, a scroll after lunch, messages picking up at five.

How it works · 02

A recap, not a spreadsheet.

Each evening, a few honest sentences about your day — written on your Mac, by a model that runs on your Mac. It never leaves. Prefer no AI? A plain template recap says the same thing in plainer words.

Privacy

Nothing leaves this machine.

UsageOS reads your window titles and the current browser tab to understand your day, and writes it to a database on your Mac. There is no network in the data path at all — and because it’s open source, you can read the code and confirm that yourself.

It never uses Screen Recording. It skips incognito and private windows, ignores anything you mark private, and auto-excludes password managers and banking. No cloud, no account, no telemetry. Ever.
